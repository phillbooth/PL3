Yes — the best approach is **not** “make whole areas enterprise”, but:

> Keep the basic version free/open.
> Make the advanced governance, compliance, automation and managed-control layers enterprise.

That keeps developer adoption strong while still protecting commercial value.

# Simple rule

Make enterprise-only anything that involves:

```text
teams
governance
compliance
SSO
audit evidence
production hardening
multi-cloud automation
central dashboards
certified packages
managed services
support contracts
```

Keep free/open anything that helps:

```text
individual developers
small teams
local development
learning
basic app building
basic local agents
basic security awareness
```

That gives LogicN the best commercial path:

> Free LogicN gets adoption.
> Enterprise LogicN sells governance, compliance, deployment safety and operational control.
